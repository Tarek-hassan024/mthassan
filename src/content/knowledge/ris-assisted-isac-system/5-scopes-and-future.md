## The Future Scope of RIS-ISAC

While the theoretical foundation is solid, there is massive scope for future research and engineering before RIS-Assisted ISAC can be deployed globally in the 2030s.

### 1. Space-Air-Ground Integrated Networks (SAGIN)
The ultimate vision of 6G is not just covering cities, but the entire planet. 
**The Scope:** Integrating RIS panels onto Low Earth Orbit (LEO) satellites and high-altitude drones (UAVs). A drone coated in RIS metamaterials could fly over a disaster zone, acting as a dynamic mirror to bounce ISAC signals from a satellite down to the ground, simultaneously providing emergency internet and radar-mapping the rubble to find survivors.

### 2. Holographic MIMO Surfaces
Current RIS panels use discrete, separated patches (usually spaced at half a wavelength). 
**The Scope:** Moving towards **Holographic Radio**. This involves creating ultra-dense surfaces where the metamaterial elements are practically continuous (spaced at fractions of a millimeter). This allows the surface to generate and project electromagnetic holograms, achieving near-perfect spatial resolution for ISAC.

### 3. THz-Band ISAC
While mmWave is the current focus, the Terahertz band (300 GHz - 3 THz) offers hundreds of gigahertz of unused bandwidth.
**The Scope:** Building RIS panels capable of operating at THz frequencies. At these frequencies, the radar resolution becomes so incredibly sharp that an ISAC base station could theoretically perform non-invasive medical imaging or scan the chemical composition of the air for pollution!

### 4. Overcoming the "Sensing Channel" Acquisition
To intelligently bounce a signal to a target, the RIS needs to know where the target is. But to find the target, the RIS needs to bounce a signal to it! This is a classic "chicken and egg" problem.
**The Scope:** Developing autonomous "Channel Estimation" protocols where the RIS can do a blind "sweeping" scan of the environment incredibly fast, without requiring the central Base Station to waste precious milliseconds doing the math.
